# MONETIZATION PLAN

## Purpose

This document defines how `typingtrainer` could make money without breaking its strongest product qualities.

`typingtrainer` is currently a proof project for `automated_company`, not the flagship company product. This plan is exploratory and does not change the current role of the repo as public proof rather than the main company direction.

The core rule is simple:

- the free product must remain real
- the paid product must feel additive, not punitive

## Monetization Principles

Rules:

- do not cripple the local-first core
- do not paywall basic lesson progression
- do not force accounts just to use the trainer
- do not collect more user data than needed for billing or optional sync
- do not turn monetization into leaderboard spam or dark patterns

## Product Packaging

Recommended packaging:

1. Free
2. Pro
3. Team / Education

## Free Plan

The free tier should remain generous enough to prove the product.

Include:

- guided lessons
- adaptive practice
- free practice
- coding practice basics
- shortcut practice basics
- local profiles
- local stats
- local export/import
- offline-capable usage after first load

Reason:

- this is the acquisition engine
- this product works because users can feel the value directly
- a weak free tier will hurt trust and growth

## Pro Plan

The Pro tier should unlock convenience, depth, and progression support.

Recommended Pro features:

- cloud sync across devices
- synced backup and restore
- premium lesson packs
- deeper coding and workflow packs
- advanced long-term analytics and reports
- structured training programs
- hosted AI-generated drills and coaching
- priority new content

The Pro tier should feel like:

- "I want more depth and convenience"

not:

- "I can barely use the product without paying"

## Team / Education Plan

This tier should come later, after the individual product is stable.

Potential features:

- seat management
- org billing
- cohort dashboards
- shared lesson packs
- instructor/admin views
- progress summaries

This is a separate packaging motion, not just a bigger Pro plan.

## Pricing Strategy

Recommended first public pricing:

- Pro monthly: EUR 9 to EUR 12
- Pro annual: EUR 79 to EUR 99

Recommended launch path:

1. public beta: free
2. founding Pro offer during beta
3. public Pro pricing at paid launch

The annual plan should be the default recommendation.

## Founding Offer

During beta or early paid rollout:

- offer a discounted annual founding plan
- keep it simple
- avoid complicated discount ladders

Purpose:

- reward early adopters
- create initial revenue
- validate willingness to pay

## Billing Recommendation

Recommended first billing provider:

- Paddle

Reason:

- simpler fit for a small software product selling internationally
- Merchant of Record positioning reduces tax/compliance burden

Alternative:

- Stripe, if later billing complexity or custom control becomes a core requirement

## Revenue Sequence

### Stage 1: No Monetization

Goal:

- validate activation and retention

Focus:

- free public beta

### Stage 2: Founding Pro

Goal:

- validate willingness to pay

Focus:

- annual founding offer
- a few clearly valuable premium features

### Stage 3: Public Pro Launch

Goal:

- build reliable recurring revenue

Focus:

- monthly and annual plans
- clearer premium packaging
- stable billing operations

### Stage 4: Team / Education

Goal:

- expand average contract value

Focus:

- seats, cohort workflows, org billing

## Conversion Drivers

The strongest likely conversion drivers are:

- cross-device sync
- deeper premium lesson packs
- advanced analytics over time
- structured training programs
- hosted AI drills and coaching

Weaker conversion drivers:

- cosmetic-only perks
- fake scarcity
- shallow gamification extras

## Retention Drivers

What should keep users paying:

- real weekly improvement
- useful sync across devices
- new lesson packs
- long-term analytics
- structured programs
- strong shortcut and coding packs

## What Not To Monetize

Avoid monetizing:

- local-only usage
- basic export/import
- core guided lessons
- basic adaptive practice
- the entire correction/symbol foundation path

Those are too central to product trust.

## Metrics

Track:

- free-to-paid conversion
- annual vs monthly share
- trial-to-paid conversion if trials are used
- churn at 30/60/90 days
- expansion into premium packs
- refund rate
- support load per paying user

## Pricing Risks

Main risks:

- pricing too early before retention is proven
- making the free tier feel crippled
- charging for features users do not yet perceive as valuable
- underpricing support-heavy plans

## Experiments

Safe experiments later:

- annual-first checkout
- founding-plan waitlist
- premium pack bundles
- team pilot pricing

Avoid early:

- too many tiers
- lifetime plans
- aggressive trial gating

## Recommendation

The practical monetization path should be:

1. keep the core trainer free
2. validate public retention
3. launch Pro for sync, premium packs, and advanced analytics
4. use Paddle first for billing
5. add team/education packaging only after the individual paid plan is stable
